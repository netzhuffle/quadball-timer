# IQA 2024 timing and scoring coverage

## Purpose and method

This inventory compares the current Quadball Timer implementation with the timing and scoring responsibilities in the **IQA Rulebook 2024, Updated November 2024**. The primary source is the supplied [`IQARulebook2024.pdf`](</Users/jannis/Library/Mobile Documents/iCloud~com~apple~iBooks/Documents/IQARulebook2024.pdf>). Page references below use the rulebook's printed page numbers, which match the PDF page numbers for the cited material.

The comparison is intentionally limited to behavior a timer/scorekeeper application can reasonably support. Physical goal/catch validity, player positioning, whistle technique, and referee judgment remain human-official responsibilities. A button for recording a confirmed event is therefore not considered deficient merely because it does not adjudicate the physical play. SQM operational requirements confirmed by the product owner - especially correction, reversibility, and Head Referee-directed overrides - carry the same acceptance weight as the rule-derived rows below.

Status meanings:

- **Correct** - the current model and controller workflow support the rule requirement, subject to the official operating the controls at the prescribed whistle or confirmation.
- **Incorrect** - the application implements the area, but current behavior can produce a rule-inconsistent result.
- **Missing** - no state, command, workflow, or safeguard covers the requirement.

## Bottom line

The core count-up game clock, stoppage coupling, ordinary 10-point goals, 20-minute seeker release, confirmed 30-point flag catch, one-minute/two-minute penalty timing, score-triggered penalty priority, and one-minute team timeout are covered well.

The app is not yet sufficient as a complete IQA 2024 official-clock and scorekeeper system. The highest-priority acceptance gaps are:

1. Store, display, and enforce the overtime target score calculated at the catch.
2. Implement heat-stoppage configuration, triggers, skip rules, and stoppage timers.
3. Make suspension records include ball possession, not only clock/score/penalties.
4. Represent penalty timing exceptions that cannot be reconstructed from the current generic card command.
5. Guarantee exclusive timekeeper control of the game clock and suppress any displayed clock that is no longer official, without unnecessarily making all scorekeeping actions exclusive.
6. Make accepted actions correctable and reversible, repair derived state after earlier corrections, and record Head Referee-directed overrides without blocking live operation.

### Operational resilience, corrections, and overrides

These are SQM production requirements confirmed by the product owner on 12 July 2026. They intentionally go beyond happy-path rule enforcement: the app assists human officials and must remain usable when information arrives late, an entry is wrong, or the Head Referee directs a non-standard continuation.

| Requirement | Status | Authority | Repository evidence | Acceptance consequence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Correct or take back every accepted control action without rebuilding the game manually. | **Incorrect** | SQM operational requirement. | The app has narrow undo paths for the latest ordinary goal and an active timeout, plus direct clock adjustment, but no general correction for cards, flag catches, overtime transitions, concessions, forfeits, suspensions, or resolved penalty expirations ([`src/lib/game-types.ts:132`](../../src/lib/game-types.ts#L132), [`src/lib/game-engine.ts:262`](../../src/lib/game-engine.ts#L262), [`src/lib/game-engine.ts:320`](../../src/lib/game-engine.ts#L320)). | Every accepted action needs an explicit correction/reversal path. ## Recommended implementation order

1. **Correction and override foundation:** choose event semantics and Controller interactions for corrections, reversals, derived-state repair, and Official Overrides so later features do not add incompatible one-off undo paths.
2. **Overtime target-score correctness:** add persisted target state, compute it from the adjudicated score at the catch, display it everywhere, repair it when an earlier score is corrected, validate completion, and fix tied concession.
3. **Heat stoppages:** add event-controlled activation, persistent trigger/skip state, dedicated 4:00/2:00 timers, official-confirmed end-of-drive activation, and early-end/extension overrides.
4. **Official-clock authority:** isolate exclusive clock-control authority within the collaborative Controller model and define when connected and offline displays remain official versus must blank.
5. **Suspension completeness:** record ball possession alongside the already-persisted clock, score, and penalties, with correction support for mistaken entries.
6. **Penalty exceptions and scorecard completeness:** add foul-before-score linkage, deferred seeker penalties, optional box-presence tracking, and card reasons according to the intended tournament role of the product.

## Explicit uncertainties and operating assumptions

- The rulebook does not prescribe clock display precision, count-up/countdown direction, an audible game-clock countdown, or a separate overtime clock. Those are not treated as gaps.
- "All other time clocks" in stoppage rules includes game-associated clocks such as penalty time. The timeout minute begins only after stoppage procedures are complete, so it correctly runs while game time is paused.
- Heat triggers occur when the game clock reaches the threshold, but the referee generally waits for the end of the drive. A compliant implementation should notify at the threshold and wait for official confirmation rather than automatically pausing play.
- Physical goal/catch validity and closely timed-event ordering are referee decisions. The app should record the ruling, not attempt to derive it from sensors or client timestamps.
- Rule-derived guards are defaults, not a substitute for live officiating authority. A Head Referee-directed deviation must remain possible through a deliberate, visible, auditable Official Override.
- Whether box-presence tracking and foul reasons are required product features depends on whether Quadball Timer is intended to replace the full official scorecard/penalty-box record or only assist those officials. They are still rulebook coverage gaps in the current application.
